How To Use Metric Time

    To use metric time, you must understand a few things first. Each unit of time is called a chron (from the Greek chronos meaning time) and is abbreviated as c. In each day there are 10 hectochron. Like the metric system, metric time uses prefixes to denote place which means there is one Kilochron in a day. 1 chron is approximately 86 seconds or a little over a minute. If you look at the clock in the top right of your screen, you may see a number like 455c. This stands for 455 chron or 4 hectochron, 5 decachron and 5 chron.
